## Anti-patterns verdict

This does not look overtly AI-generated. The restrained warm-neutral system, Inter typography, compact shell, and shared primitives feel credible for a desktop tool. The risk is genericness: the recurring muted-card/list pattern offers little product-specific identity or task guidance.

The deterministic scan found two warnings: `overused-font` in `src/overlay/overlay.css:27`, and `layout-transition` in `src/overlay/overlay.css:158`. Both are low-priority / likely false positives for the reviewed main surface: the first is overlay-only and the second animates a 2px audio meter bar. Browser review found no console errors, but the document title remains `vite-app` (`index.html:7`) and duplicate controls named “Toggle Sidebar” appear in the accessibility tree.

## Overall impression

The foundation is good: calm, consistent, and already more considered than a typical early desktop app. The biggest opportunity is to turn Home from a retrospective of zero metrics into a confident pre-flight surface for the next dictation.

## What is working

- Shared shell and page/list primitives keep screens coherent (`src/components/app-shell.tsx`, `src/components/page.tsx`).
- Domain language is generally plain and task-oriented: snippets explain their invocation phrase, and settings labels explain technical choices.
- Loading, error, and empty states appear across Home, Snippets, and Insights instead of rendering blank content.

## Priority issues

1. **[P1] Home lacks a primary dictation action and readiness signal.** It shows stats and an inert empty state (`src/pages/home-page.tsx:67-85`) but not the configured shortcut, microphone/model status, or how to start. Make the hero a compact readiness panel with the shortcut, mic/model/insertion status, and “Hold [shortcut] to dictate”; move history beneath it.
2. **[P1] Snippet deletion is irreversible and hover-only.** The delete action directly calls the mutation (`src/pages/snippets-page.tsx:238-244`). Provide an undo toast (preferred) or contextual confirmation for used snippets; keep the action keyboard-visible when the row receives focus.
3. **[P2] Settings replaces the product navigation.** Settings conditionally swaps the sidebar content (`src/components/app-sidebar.tsx:36-41`), forcing a context switch. Preserve the main nav or give settings a persistent breadcrumb/back path.
4. **[P2] Logs exposes too much at once.** Six filters plus pause/clear and capture/testing actions are visible together (`src/pages/logs-page.tsx:352-405`). Collapse filters, split passive viewing from test capture, and make diagnostics explicitly advanced.
5. **[P2] Browser fallback is visually partial.** The fallback renders native-only controls in an ambiguous state. Use one clear “Available in the desktop app” notice and consistently disable or hide native-only actions.

## Persona red flags

- **Alex (power user):** The configured shortcut and readiness state are buried in settings, making the daily path slower than it should be.
- **Jordan (first-timer):** “No dictations yet” explains where history will appear, not how to create the first dictation (`src/pages/home-page.tsx:79-84`).
- **Sam (accessibility-dependent):** `PushToTalkButton` only provides mouse/touch press handlers (`src/components/dictation-demo.tsx:214-235`); destructive snippet actions are visually hidden until hover. Global `select-none` (`src/index.css:195-208`) also makes transcript copying an opt-in rather than the default.

## Minor observations

- The app title and favicon are still Vite defaults (`index.html:5-7`).
- The shell header and page header repeat route titles.
- Custom scrollbar styling (`src/index.css:233-269`) adds brand treatment without improving the task.
- Warm gray is cohesive but needs clearer semantic differentiation for active, informative, and ready states.

## Questions to consider

- Should Home be a pre-flight control center rather than an activity dashboard?
- Is Logs a regular product feature, or a support/developer workflow that should stay out of the primary navigation?
